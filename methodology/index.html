Methodology

Overview

Our calculation of the importance of a vote is simply the product of the two following factors:

  1. The vote weight: The number of electoral votes per voter
  2. The ambiguity factor: The probability of the state swinging in the election

This score is then provided as ordinal data, as its actual numeric value has no intrinsic meaning.

Calculating Vote Weight

The vote weight calculation is simple: We divide the number of electoral votes in the state by the number of active voters in the state.

We calculate the number of active voters in the state by using a simple least-squares linear regression model based off of data from the past 5 presidential elections gathered from the US Census Bureau. Intuitively, the vote weight refers to a general likelihood for your vote to swing the election based solely off of the number of voters in your state.

Calculating the Ambiguity Factor

The ambiguity factor represents the likelihood that a vote will swing in an election. The formula for this calculation is based off of polling data for each state.
